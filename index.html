The quick guide

Yes please, and no thanks

When in doubt, leave it out — one wrong item can contaminate a whole truckload. Here are the everyday ones.

✓

Goes in FOGO

  • Fruit & vegetable scraps
  • Meat, bones & seafood shells
  • Dairy, eggshells & bread
  • Tea leaves & coffee grounds
  • Lawn clippings, leaves & weeds
  • Small branches & prunings
  • Soiled paper towel & newspaper
✕

Keep these out

  • Plastic bags & soft plastics
  • "Compostable" or bioplastic liners*
  • Glass, metal & recyclables
  • Nappies & wet wipes
  • Soil, rocks & rubble
  • Treated or painted timber
  • Pet waste & kitty litter

*Many councils no longer accept compostable liners — check yours. Only lined caddies using council-issued lime-green liners may be permitted.

From kerb to compost

Where it actually goes

Your bin isn't the end of the line — it's the start of a roughly eight-week journey back into living soil.

🪣

Caddy & bin

Scraps collect in your kitchen caddy, then empty into the lime-green bin.

🚛

Collected

Picked up kerbside and trucked to a local composting facility.

🌡️

Composted

Aerated and heated for weeks — microbes break it down and kill pathogens.

🌱

Back to earth

Screened into rich compost for farms, parks and gardens.
